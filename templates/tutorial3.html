Understanding the Risk Score

After processing a loan application, CreditMetrics provides a comprehensive risk assessment. At the heart of this assessment is the risk scoreâ€”a numerical value that represents the probability of default.

The risk score is displayed in two complementary formats:

  • Default Probability: A percentage between 0% and 100% indicating the likelihood that the loan will default
  • Risk Category: A simplified classification into Low, Medium, or High risk bands
Low Risk
0-10%
Default probability is minimal. These applications represent excellent credit risks.

11-30%

Note: Risk thresholds can be customized for your bank's specific risk tolerance in the account settings.
2

Exploring the Risk Report

Each analysis generates a detailed risk report that provides context and explanation for the risk assessment. Understanding the components of this report will help you make informed decisions.

Key sections of the risk report include:

1. Executive Summary

A concise overview of the risk assessment, including the final risk score, confidence level, and primary risk factors. This section provides a quick snapshot for decision-makers.

2. Contributing Factors

Identifies the specific attributes that contributed most significantly to the risk assessment, both positively and negatively. This helps you understand why a particular score was assigned.

For example, a report might indicate that a high credit score significantly reduced risk, while a high debt-to-income ratio increased risk.

3. Confidence Analysis

Indicates how confident the system is in its prediction. Higher confidence scores (closer to 100%) suggest more reliable predictions. Lower confidence might indicate unusual applicant profiles or missing data.

4. Comparative Context

Shows how this applicant compares to similar profiles in the historical database. This provides context for understanding whether the risk level is typical for this type of application.

3

Confidence Intervals and Uncertainty

No prediction is 100% certain. CreditMetrics provides confidence intervals to help you understand the range of possible outcomes and the reliability of each prediction.

The confidence interval represents the range within which the true default probability is likely to fall. For example:

  • A prediction of 15% default probability with a confidence interval of Â±3% means the true risk is likely between 12% and 18%
  • A wider confidence interval (e.g., Â±8%) indicates greater uncertainty in the prediction

Factors that influence confidence intervals include:

  • Data completeness: More complete applicant profiles yield narrower confidence intervals
  • Historical precedent: Common applicant profiles have narrower intervals than unusual ones
  • Contradictory signals: When different attributes suggest conflicting risk levels, uncertainty increases
Important: Applications with high uncertainty may warrant additional manual review, even if the predicted risk is low.

Risk-Based Decision Making

CreditMetrics provides risk assessments, but the final lending decision remains with your bank. Here are recommended strategies for incorporating risk assessments into your decision-making:

Risk Category Recommended Action Additional Considerations
Low Risk (0-10%) Approve with standard terms Consider preferential rates for extremely low-risk applications
Medium Risk (11-30%) Conditional approval Consider:
  • Additional collateral
  • Higher down payment
  • Shorter loan term
  • Risk-based pricing
High Risk (31-100%) Elevated scrutiny or denial If considering approval:
  • Require substantial guarantees
  • Apply significant risk premiums
  • Verify all information thoroughly
  • Consider smaller initial loan amount

Many banks implement hybrid decision processes:

  • Automated approvals: Very low-risk applications are automatically approved
  • Automated denials: Very high-risk applications are automatically denied
  • Manual review: Applications in the middle range or with unusual profiles receive human review
5

Batch Analysis and Portfolio Risk

Beyond individual applications, CreditMetrics enables portfolio-level risk analysis. When conducting batch analyses, you receive additional insights:

  • Risk distribution: See how risk is distributed across your loan portfolio
  • Risk concentration: Identify whether risk is concentrated in specific segments
  • Expected default rate: Calculate the aggregate expected default rate across the portfolio
  • Risk factors analysis: Discover common risk factors affecting your customer base

These portfolio insights help with strategic planning, including:

  • Setting appropriate interest rates based on risk profiles
  • Adjusting marketing and outreach to target preferred risk segments
  • Planning for potential default scenarios
  • Making capital reserve decisions

Exporting and Integrating Results

CreditMetrics offers multiple ways to export and integrate risk assessment results into your existing systems:

Export Options

  • PDF Reports: Comprehensive reports suitable for documentation and regulatory purposes
  • CSV/Excel: Tabular data for further analysis and integration with spreadsheet tools
  • JSON/API: Structured data for direct integration with your banking systems

Integration Points

Common integration scenarios include:

  • Incorporating risk scores directly into your loan processing system
  • Feeding risk data into your customer relationship management (CRM) platform
  • Adding risk insights to management dashboards
  • Including risk assessments in regulatory reporting
